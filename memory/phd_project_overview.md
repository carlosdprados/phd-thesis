# PhD Project: Organic Memristive Devices via Ionic Migration in Polymer Composites

**Institution:** Universitat de València, Institute of Molecular Science (ICMol), Nanotech group  
**Supervisor:** Salvador Cardona-Serra (PI: Eugenio Coronado)  
**Duration:** 2020–2025 (active, ongoing as of 2026-04)  
**Funding:** Spanish MINECO PRE2020-093666 predoctoral grant; ERC-AdG MOL2D 788222; ERC AdG HELD 834431; Excellence Unit María de Maeztu CEX2019-000919-M

## Research Focus
Design, fabrication, and characterisation of two-terminal (2-T) organic memristive devices based on ion-transport polymer composites. The devices exploit ionic migration within a polymer matrix to produce tunable, reversible resistance states, emulating synaptic behaviour for neuromorphic computing.

## Key Outputs
- **Paper 1 (published):** "Polymer-Based Composites for Engineering Organic Memristive Devices", Prado-Socorro et al., *Adv. Electron. Mater.* **2022**, 8, 2101192. DOI: 10.1002/aelm.202101192
- **Patent (granted):** "Dispositivos Memristivos por Migración Iónica", ES 2927156 B2, granted 24 March 2023
- **Conferences:** MEMRISYS 2021 (Japan), EMRS Spring 2021, ENMM 2022 (Vigo), MEMRISYS 2023 (Turin), ECSAMM 2025

## Core Materials System
- **Active layer composite:** Super Yellow (SY, PPV-based semiconducting polymer) + Hybrane DEO750 8500 (ion-transport polymer, branched polyester) + ionic salt (LiCF₃SO₃, or NaBis, KBis in later generations)
- **Bottom electrode:** ITO on glass
- **Top electrode:** Ag (100 nm, thermally evaporated); Au used in earlier/comparison devices
- **Active area:** 0.0825 cm²
- **Film thickness:** ~209 nm (at 2000 rpm)

## Device Generations
- 300+ device versions tracked as NM_vXXX in Nanomem_Devices_Library
- Latest (2025-Q2): NM_v334–v338 with TMPELiBis, TMPENaBis variants at Ag electrode
- Systematic variation of: ionic species (Li, Na, K), concentration, spin speed, annealing temperature, electrode material

## Why: Motivation
The von Neumann bottleneck limits AI hardware scalability. Neuromorphic in-memory computing requires analog, multi-state, reversible resistance devices. Organic materials offer tunability, biocompatibility, and low cost vs. inorganic counterparts.

## How to apply
When writing thesis chapters or planning experiments, anchor all discussion to this composite system. The evolution from Li-only (Paper 1) to Li/Na/K comparison (Ag electrodes) is the narrative arc of the thesis.
